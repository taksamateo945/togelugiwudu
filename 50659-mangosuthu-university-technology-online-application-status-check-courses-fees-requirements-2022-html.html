Mangosuthu University of Technology

The Mangosuthu University of Technology establishment was an idea that was fronted by Dr Mangosuthu Buthelezi, the chief minister of KwaZulu Natal. He came up with the idea after seeing the need for South Africa to train and employ more technicians. The institution started as Mangosuthu Technikon, and in November 2007, it was renamed the Mangosuthu University of Technology.

How can I apply to Mangosuthu University?

Before proceeding with the application process, any South African prospectus student needs to ensure they have the following application documents:

  • Identity Document
  • National Senior Certificate or Statement of Results
  • Senior Certificate
  • Students transferring from other universities should submit an original academic record and a certificate of good conduct.

Non-South African prospectus students are required to have the following documents:

  • A valid passport
  • A valid study permit
  • A valid medical aid/health insurance cover
  • GCE, GCSE, IGCSE or Cambridge School Certificate
  • Students holding a GCE GCSE IGCSE or the Cambridge School Certificate must have passed five approved ordinary level subjects, including the English language, provided that three subjects have been passed at the same examination sitting.

Is Mangosuthu University of Technology open for 2022 applications?

The university allows for applications strictly through the Central Application Office. It is a simplified way of applying for more than one programme via one application form and paying the application fee once. Prospectus students are advised to select the Mangosuthu University of Technology as a first choice.

Resident applicants are required to enter details of their Identification documents, while non-residents are required to enter details of their passports.

What are MUT requirements?

Every faculty stipulates admission requirements depending on the points that a prospective student has in their best five subjects. Other departments require a student to sit a mandatory entrance test.

These are the minimum entrance requirements:

  • National Senior Certificate with a diploma pass or N3 with 50% pass in Mathematics and English
  • A minimum of 5 subjects with level 4 pass for diploma study and level 3 for Access Courses
  • English Home Language or First Additional Language, level 4 pass
  • Specific subject combination and pass level relevant to the qualification
  • Minimum points as per faculty requirements and as listed on the CAO handbook

Meeting these minimum entry requirements does not guarantee entry into the institution due to limited space. The selection process prioritizes the best-qualified applicants and those who selected the Mangosuthu University of Technology as their first choice.

MUT online application

Application forms are available on the Central Application Office website. However, a prospectus student can apply to the university online through the Central Application Office website. The website provides the apply tab, and clicking on it directs you to the application process. The CAO number appears in the application form on the tear-off slip. The CAO number is required while paying for the application fees.

Mangosuthu University of Technology status check

If you are an applicant, you can track the progress of your application through the CAO website by entering your CAO number. Alternatively, you could call 031 268 4444 or email enqgeneralcao@cao.ac.za.

The status check process will require you to present the following credentials:

  • CAO number
  • Identity number
  • Passport number

Is Mangosuthu University of Technology open for 2022 application?

Is MUT still open for applications? The management of the Mangosuthu University of Technology has made the admission portal accessible to prospectus students. The university has also rolled out specific dates and deadlines for admission registration and closure of the admission portal.

Most South African institutions of higher learning have their application deadline within August and October. This is not the case with the Mangosuthu University of Technology. Limited MUT admission slots are available; therefore, prospectus students are advised to apply early. Late applications will not be considered.

Mangosuthu University of Technology online application 2022 closing date

Meanwhile, these are the deadlines for the 2022/2023 admission registration:

  • Faculty of Engineering – first semester applications – 30th September 2022
  • Faculty of Engineering – second-semester applications – 31st May 2022
  • Faculty of Natural Sciences – 30th September 2022
  • Analytical Chemistry – second-semester applications – 31st May 2022.
  • Faculty of Management Sciences – 30th September 2022.

MUT late applications

All applications submitted after 30th September 2022 accumulate a late application fee determined by the Central Application Office. Meanwhile, the Mangosuthu University of Technology Application portal is expected to shut down on the same date at 11:59 PM.

Which courses are still available at MUT for 2022?

Which courses are still available at MUT? According to the MUT application deadlines, the university is open for first semester applications in the faculty of Engineering, natural sciences and management sciences. Prospectus students are advised to submit their applications before the deadline.

Mangosuthu University of Technology fees

Did you know you can afford to study at MUT? The university offers the most competitive tuition fee structure. The Mangosuthu University of Technology fees range between R4,530 and R15,350 per semester. The tuition fees depend on the course and subjects one is pursuing. Meanwhile, residence fees range between R4,120 and R11,150 per semester. The tuition and residence fees do not include travel expenses, stationery, meals and textbooks.
